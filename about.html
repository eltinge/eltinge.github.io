About

I grew up in the Washington, DC metro area and moved to Providence, RI in 2013 to attend Brown University. I graduated in 2017 with a double major in public health and statistics. My public health honors thesis, which focused on personalizing treatments for alcohol use disorder, was recognized as the best undergraduate thesis in the School of Public Health. You can read a bit more about it here. At Brown, I was also heavily involved in the Meiklejohn peer advising program and Environmental Program House.

Professionally, I’m interested in applying my data science skills to real-world public health problems. I believe that improving environmental and behavioral health will be key to the success of future generations, and I see a big role for data science to play in this work. If you have ideas for open-source projects in these and other social justice areas, please reach out!

I’m proud to be a transplanted Rhode Islander and will gladly tell you about our beaches, local food scene, and small-state quirks. In my free time, I enjoy knitting, trivia competitions, and cooking vegetarian meals with local produce from Veggie Box RI. I also recently started fostering kittens through the Providence Animal Rescue League.
